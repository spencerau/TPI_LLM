# Job Interview Preparation Checklist

## Checklist:

- Review the job description.
- Understand the company mission and goals - Understand them from multiple perspectives ex. big picture to small picture.
- Review business website - researching the company job description and the interviewer.
- Practice common interview questions: general, situational, behavioral, and if applicable technical questions that pertain to your industry. (refer to links at the end)
  - Sell yourself, don't be modest about your accomplishments. How you deliver results.
  - "Tell me about yourself." - 2-3min professional summary about who you are and where you are in your "career path".
  - Behavioral-based questions "Tell me about when..." "Give me an example of when..."
    - Situation, Task, Action, Result (STAR)
  - High-pressure questions - examples:
    - Why you are leaving your company? Why there is a gap in your resume?
    - What salary are you looking for?
    - A momentary pause to think is ok.
- Prepare questions to ask in return - the last 5 min of the interview.
  - 2-3 questions.
    - What does a typical day look like?
    - How would you describe the company culture?
    - What is the next step in the hiring process?
  - Clarify concerns you have; keep professional.
- Material - consider bringing a printed resume, pen, and paper.
- Present yourself professionally. Plan and try on your interview outfit - aim to dress 1 level above employees there.
- Expect the unexpected - perform a technology check a day or more in advance if the interview is virtually online.Mock Interview - with career coaches or anyone helpful to give honest feedback. Imitate the environment - dress up. Off-site location if applicable. Record the session and critique it.

At the interview:

Be punctual - arrive 15-30 minutes early.  
 Convey confidence - convey confidence in what you say, and do pay attention to how you're presenting yourself, with your body language, and with your tone of voice to ensure that you're coming across as confident, poised, and prepared for the role.  
 Mindset - you evaluate them as much as they are evaluating you.
